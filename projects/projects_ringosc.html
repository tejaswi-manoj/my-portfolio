    • Ring Oscillator

    ANALOG AND MIXED-SIGNAL DESIGN •
    Cadence Virtuoso

    Challenge

    Build a ring oscillator with a buffer and a load of 1 pF capacitance.

    Background

    This was an onboarding project I completed to become a member of the Analog and Mixed-Signal Design Subteam at GT's premier chip design organization.

    Approach

    These were the requirements given:

    Name Min Max Unit
    VDD -- 1.8 V
    GND -- 0 V
    Oscillator Power -- 1 mW
    Load Capacitance -- 1 pF
    Oscillation Frequency 450 550 MHz
    Temperature 0 70 °C

    For a 3-stage ring oscillator, f = 1 / (2·N·tp), where N is the number of inverter stages and tp is the propagation delay per stage. If we are targetting a range 450–550 MHz meant that we need a a tp of roughly 303–370 ps.

    Load capacitors at each inverter output allow us to tune that delay, since tp = CL·VDD / Idriv. Propagation delay scales directly with load capacitance. Starting from C ≈ 17.36 fF (tp ≈ 219.27 ps), scaling by ~1.6× to C ≈ 27.74 fF brought the delay into range.

    The 1 pF output load was too large for the oscillator to drive directly, so a tapered inverter buffer chain stepped up the drive strength, with each stage roughly 2× the size of the one before it with Stage 1 driving Stage 2, Stage 2 driving Stage 3, Stage 3 driving Stage 4, and Stage 4 driving the 1 pF load.
